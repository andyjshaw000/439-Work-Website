Here I went with 300 ohms for the LEDs since I knew the Arduino provided 5 volts and the circuits were in series. The LEDs are recommended to be
powered around 20 mA, so 5/.02 = 250 ohms and 330 ohms was the closest we had. I did not want to go lower than 250 ohms, as I did not want to possibly
short my LEDs. I went with a 10k ohm resistor for my switch to avoid an open circuit and to keep the pin grounded when the switch is closed.
